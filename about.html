The Economy and Color Use is a research-driven project by Miranda Chalbaud Acosta that investigates how luxury fashion brands might subconsciously or strategically adapt their use of color, particularly red,in response to economic conditions.

Leveraging data science, color psychology, and fashion theory, this project combines runway imagery analysis with key financial indicators like GDP growth and consumer confidence. The goal is to understand whether aesthetic decisions on the runway reflect broader societal moods and economic realities.

All data was collected from publicly available sources such as Vogue Runway and economic databases including the Federal Reserve and World Bank.

This project was developed as part of a broader exploration into how design choices intersect with cultural and economic shifts. Special thanks to open-source tools like Python, OpenCV, and Flourish Studio for enabling creative data storytelling.
