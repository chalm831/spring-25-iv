The Economy and Color Use

Can this shade be the answer to economic downturns?

More than ever brands and their teams are trying to discover the secret ingredient to success. With an ever changing socioeconomic landscape becoming resilient is one of the most important assets that can be developed. Color isn’t just a matter of aesthetics in fashion; it’s a tool of communication, emotion, and even business. This project explores whether there’s a possible relationship between economic uncertainty and the use of color in luxury fashion.

We hypothesize: Luxury fashion brands use more red during economic downturns to evoke urgency, power, and stimulate consumer response.

Using runway images from brands like Valentino, Gucci, and Chanel, combined with economic data, this project analyzes how often red takes center stage when markets decline. Through color extraction techniques and data visualization, patterns begin to emerge. Suggesting that fashion doesn’t just follow trends, it responds to the mood of the moment, and perhaps, tries to shift it.

In times of economic grey, does luxury turn to red?
This project explores if and asks why.
